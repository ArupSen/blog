It's the 3rd of July and really the first time since my house move that I am sitting down at my computer to work on my goals. As I mentioned in the previous report, even though I haven't been formally scrutinising my goals and looking at the details in the way that I usually do, I have still been pursuing them, so all has not been lost. But it's about time to get back to my original plan for the year, which will also be reviewed and updated since another 3 months have passed. But what about July? What do I want to work on and achieve in July? Is it time to remove redundant goals that no longer apply and perhaps add new ones that have been knocking at the door waiting to be let in?

We fast forward to the 19th of July. The school holidays are about to start and summer is well under way. I am starting to feel settled at my new home after about 2 months or so. Today we had a new boiler installed which took up most of the day. I got on with odd jobs and fasted. I'm sort of in touch with my goals and sort of not. I'm in touch with them in the sense that I do think about them and almost out of habit because they haven't changed in a very long time I remember I could or should be doing such a thing. I'm not in touch with them because I haven't been looking at the year plan or my goals for the month. But it does feel like things are moving forward even though I'm not formally keeping track.

Last month was busy with all the extra yoga classes and working at the shop but now that the classes are back to their usual number it doesn't really feel less busy. I still have a lot of work at the shop and I'm still getting used to this lifestyle of separating home and work. Because it is such a long commute I try and do as much as I can while I'm there and make it a long day: a typical day would be from 10am till 10pm. I don't really pursue my goals on those days; I can't do yoga, strength training, read or work on my typing skills. I can read a little on my tube journeys but usually get distracted by emails and messages or try hard to stay awake. I get the sense that I'm more productive because when I'm there, I'm there to work rather than work interspersed with a lot of play. It's a bit like a fasting day where I am able to put off my urge to enjoy food till the next day. It's focused work and something that has been necessary due to the heavy demand of late.

At the start of July it being the end of half of the year I was meant to update the year plan and set goals for the next three months but now July is over as it's the 2nd of August so it's time to wrap things up and simply complete my monthly report. I do feel that I'm back on top of things again. I do feel a little guilty that I have missed a lot and that I should be more connected to my goals and working on them during more of my waking hours but having read through my year plan one of my main principles or guidelines for this year was to lower my expectations. It's OK. I'm OK. It'll all be fine.

  • Strength

    Goals: Re-establish 3 sets per exercise. Push rep range to 8-12. Build daily handstand practice gradually.

    I have been pushing up my rep range and have also started to do three sets of all the main exercises. It somehow feels more solid and firm in this flat. Doing the bodyline drills and handstands is much easier on carpet. I seem to be able to balance for a second or two longer in the handstands too. I haven't yet re-introduced the 'greasing the groove' technique or doing handstands every day but it does feel good to be able set a slot and stick to that slot. What I mean is that because I have been working long days at the shop in Walthamstow I have had to miss my sessions sometimes at my previous slots and have been doing only two sessions a week. But that means when I schedule a time I really have to do it at that time or it just won't happen. One time I went to Ikea and started training immediately upon my return. I also trained one time straight after coming home from a Samaritans shift. I still can't help feeling that I should be more advanced but how would that be possible if I have been having breaks from training?

    I've been very consistent with my training even though some weeks it has been only twice. I've trained while Maya has been around and she likes to join in. The reps have been going up steadily and I've been thinking a little about diet and sleep to help enhance my efforts.
